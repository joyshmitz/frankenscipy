perf: fsci-interpolate polyfit — hoist A^T A mirror out of sample loop + reuse xpow buffer
==========================================================================================

Target : crates/fsci-interpolate/src/lib.rs :: polyfit (the A^T A / A^T b normal-equations build).
Lever  : The build accumulated the upper triangle of A^T A but mirrored each entry to the lower
         triangle INSIDE the per-sample loop (`ata[k][j] = ata[j][k]` for every sample) — O(n·ncols²)
         redundant, cache-scattered writes — and allocated a fresh `xpow` Vec per sample. Fix:
         accumulate the upper triangle only, reuse one xpow buffer, mirror once after the loop.

Isomorphism proof (byte-identical)
----------------------------------
- The upper-triangle accumulation operands/order are unchanged (ata[j][k] += xpow[j]*xpow[k] over
  samples i=0..n, j<=k); the lower triangle is never READ during accumulation, so removing the
  in-loop mirror changes nothing, and the single post-loop mirror copies the same finished values
  the per-sample assignment left behind. xpow[0] is x^0=1 and is never overwritten, so the reused
  buffer yields identical xpow[j] = x[i]^j. So A^T A and A^T b — and hence the fitted coefficients
  (downstream of solve_normal) — are bit-for-bit identical.
- Empirically: 0 mismatches / 3000 random problems (deg in [1,14], n in [deg+1, deg+60], |x|<=1);
  the full A^T A and A^T b compared via .to_bits() between a verbatim original build and the new
  build. A polyfit() end-to-end call per trial feeds the golden digest.
- Golden payload sha256 (per-trial digest of the fitted coefficient vector):
    dc7067fadd5efcb524b0a73e8067ad491470b02643dbeabe169de62c351b0c57  golden_payload.txt
